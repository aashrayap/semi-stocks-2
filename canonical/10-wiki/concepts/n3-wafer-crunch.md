# N3 Wafer Crunch

N3 refers to TSMC's 3-nanometer manufacturing process — the most advanced way to make chips. Every major AI accelerator is moving to N3 simultaneously in 2026, creating a capacity crunch.

## Why It's a Bottleneck

- AI already consumes 60% of TSMC N3 output (2026), projected 86% by 2027
- N3 utilization at 100%+ in H2 2026
- TSMC Q4 2025 printed $33.73B revenue and 62.3% gross margin, then guided Q1 to $34.6B-$35.8B on strong leading-edge demand
- TSMC 3nm already accounted for 28% of wafer revenue, while advanced nodes were 77% of wafer revenue
- Nvidia signaled demand earlier than Google or Amazon and locked more upstream wafer and memory capacity
- Intel said revenue would have been materially higher with more supply, and DCAI revenue rose 15% sequentially
- All major accelerators moving to N3 simultaneously: Nvidia Rubin, Google TPU v7/v8, Amazon Trainium3, AMD MI350X
- AI chips are literally crowding out smartphone chips (Apple, Qualcomm, MediaTek)
- Apple should become a smaller share of advanced-node demand as N2 ramps and AI absorbs more allocation
- NVIDIA supply commitments doubled $50.3B → $95.2B QoQ, capacity secured through CY2027 (Q4 FY2026)
- Vera Rubin samples shipped, production H2 2026 — six new chips this cycle all on N3

## Counter-Signal

The shortage is real now, but the relief path is visible:
- TSMC's 2026 capital budget is $52B-$56B
- Intel is ramping 18A and pushing 14A customer decisions into H2 2026-H1 2027
- Both are exactly the sort of supply responses that eventually break the crunch

## Earnings Confirmation

- **CRWV Q4 2025:** Delivered 50K GB200s in Q4 to single customer. Nvidia GPUs "remain in short supply." $30-35B capex = enormous chip demand.
- **TSM Q4 2025:** $33.73B revenue, 62.3% gross margin, 3nm at 28% of wafer revenue, advanced nodes at 77%, and Q1 guide of $34.6B-$35.8B on strong leading-edge demand.
- **INTC Q4 2025:** Intel said AI-driven demand created supply constraints, DCAI revenue grew 15% sequentially, and revenue would have been higher with more supply. At the same time, 18A/14A are the future relief path.
- **NVDA Q4 FY2026:** Rubin moving to N3, competing with TPU v7/v8, Trainium3, MI350X for TSMC capacity. Supply commitments nearly doubled. Six new chips all on N3.
