# EUV Tool Bottleneck

`ASML` is retained as the long-run upstream ceiling in the reduced model.

## Why It Matters

- Tool throughput sets the upper bound on leading-edge wafer output.
- `TSM` can only expand advanced-node output as tooling and supplier cadence allows.
- `NVDA` demand intensity pulls this upstream constraint forward over time.

## Reduced-Scope Read-Through

The near-term watchpoint is N3 utilization (`TSM`/`NVDA`), while EUV remains the next-stage gate (`ASML`) when broader foundry expansion saturates.
