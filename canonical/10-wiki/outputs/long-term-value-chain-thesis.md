# Long-Term AI Hardware Value Chain Thesis

Two independent analytical frameworks — Wang's structural margin compression and Baker's cyclicality — converge on the same directional conclusion for NVIDIA but diverge on the physical infrastructure layers. This output synthesizes both into a longer-term positioning framework.

---

## The Two Frameworks

### Framework 1: Margin as Residual (Wang)

NVIDIA's gross margin is the surplus left after paying supply-constrained monopolists and before selling to an oligopsony building alternatives. It compresses from both directions simultaneously, independent of demand.

- **From below:** TSMC 5-10% annual price hikes + HBM 20%+ price increases compound against NVIDIA's BoM
- **From above:** Hyperscaler custom silicon (TPU v7, Trainium2/3, Maia, AMD MI355X) provides credible BATNA that disciplines NVIDIA pricing
- **Evidence:** GAAP gross margin already compressed 75.0% → 71.1% (FY2025 → FY2026)
- **Timeline:** Structural, ongoing. Margin compression accelerates as supplier contracts reprice and customer alternatives mature.

### Framework 2: Cyclicality (Baker)

Every semiconductor shortage is followed by a glut. Stocks lead fundamentals by 6-12 months. Multiple compression begins during the shortage phase, not after it ends. This has been confirmed across 6 subsectors and 40+ years of data.

- **Current phase:** Peak shortage. MU fwd P/E compressing to 10-12x on record earnings. NVDA fwd P/E at 21x (down from 60x+). All three memory vendors expanding simultaneously. TSMC $52-56B capex.
- **Cycle turn:** 2027-2028 if supply additions overshoot demand. Baker hedges with $2.2B QQQ puts.
- **Timeline:** Cyclical, 12-24 months from peak shortage to glut onset.

### Where They Converge

Both frameworks agree: **NVIDIA's stock is the most exposed name on a 2-3 year horizon.**

- Wang: Margin compresses even if revenue grows, because NVIDIA absorbs bilateral pressure from monopolist suppliers and an oligopsony with growing alternatives
- Baker: Multiple compresses during shortage, then revenue declines during glut — the double whammy
- Together: NVDA faces margin compression (structural) AND multiple compression (cyclical) AND eventual revenue deceleration (cyclical). Three headwinds, same stock.

## Tier 1: Highest Long-Term Conviction

### TSM — The Toll Booth

**Why it's the best longer-term position:**

Every AI chip produced globally — NVIDIA GPU, Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, AMD MI-series, every Broadcom custom ASIC — routes through TSMC fabrication and advanced packaging. NVIDIA's customers building alternatives to NVIDIA is bullish for TSMC, because those alternatives need TSMC too. Wang's substitutability ranking puts TSMC's CoWoS at #1 (zero alternatives) and logic fab at #3 (92% sub-5nm share). TSMC is the only company in the AI hardware stack that benefits from every possible competitive outcome.

**Pricing power runway:** TSMC captures $877-922 per H100 (3-4% of selling price). A 30% across-the-board increase adds only $260-280 per unit — less than 1% of GPU ASP. The repricing trajectory has years of structural room to run. Gross margins guided to 63-65% for early 2026, with 5-10% annual sub-5nm hikes announced.

**Valuation:** ~25x fwd P/E. This is a discount to the S&P 500 IT sector (~32x) for a company with 92% sub-5nm share and a packaging monopoly facing 113% demand CAGR. The market prices TSMC as a foundry cyclical when it is increasingly an infrastructure monopoly with pricing power it hasn't begun to exercise.

**Cyclical risk:** Real. TSM fell 58% in 2022-2023. Baker's framework says the stock will lead the revenue downturn by 6-18 months. But the floor is structurally higher each cycle: AI was <20% of TSMC revenue in 2022; it will be 50%+ by 2027. The trough will be shallower.

**Positioning lens:**
- Baker: Not directly positioned (too consensus?)
- Leopold: Exited TSM puts in Q4 2025
- Wang: Explicitly calls TSMC "the most underpriced monopoly in the AI hardware stack"

**Action framework:** Core position. On any cyclical drawdown (25-40%), add aggressively. The structural pricing power thesis compounds through cycles. TSM earnings on 2026-04-17 — the first opportunity to see Q1 2026 pricing power data.

### ASML — The Terminal Ceiling

**Why it belongs in Tier 1 despite near-term risk:**

ASML is the deepest upstream monopoly. Zero substitutes for EUV. Zeiss optics limits production to ~100 tools/year by 2030. One gigawatt of Rubin-class capacity needs ~3.5 EUV tools. Every fab TSMC builds, every node Intel ramps, every Samsung process depends on ASML. The EUV tool throughput is the terminal constraint that governs the entire AI semiconductor supply chain.

**Pricing power:** ASML has historically left substantial value with TSMC rather than extracting the theoretical maximum (Wang footnote 2). High-NA EUV tools at $380M+ (up from $120M in 2017) show the repricing trajectory. Multi-year backlog provides revenue visibility.

**Cyclical risk:** Equipment stocks lead the cycle by 12+ months. Bookings at record EUR 13.2B (Q4 2025). Baker's framework: this is exactly when to worry. ASML stock fell 55% in the prior cycle despite revenue continuing to grow (backlog lag). P/E hit 88x at bookings peak.

**Action framework:** Best entry is during the next bookings downturn, not now. Baker's playbook: buy equipment stocks when bookings are falling and sentiment is terrible (P/E will appear high on depressed forward estimates). On any 30-50% drawdown driven by bookings deceleration, build a core position. ASML earnings on 2026-04-16 — watch bookings closely for early cycle-turn signals.

## Tier 2: Strong Structural Thesis, Timing-Dependent

### MU (Memory) — The Re-Rating Bet

**Bull case (Wang / structural):** HBM oligopoly with 3 players controlling 95%+ of production. Long-term contracts replacing spot pricing. Revenue per wafer 3-5x conventional DRAM. If the LNG analogy holds (commodity → contracted quasi-utility), the current 5-8x fwd P/E embeds a cyclical discount the business no longer warrants. A re-rating to 12-15x would be a 50-100% move from current levels. NVIDIA's $53B in memory purchase commitments confirms structural demand.

**Bear case (Baker / cyclical):** All three vendors expanding capacity simultaneously. Micron FY2026 capex above $25B. NVIDIA $21.4B inventory = potential double-ordering signal. MU fwd P/E compressing to 10-12x on record EPS $12.20 — the classic peak signal that Baker has documented across three complete memory cycles. If the cycle turns in 2027-2028, MU falls 40-50% (historical precedent: $98 → $49 in 2022-2023).

**The question:** Has HBM structurally broken the memory cycle? Wang says yes (long-term contracts, oligopoly supply discipline, 3x wafer area per bit constrains supply). Baker says the pattern will repeat (he hedges with puts even while long). This is the single most important thesis question in the stack.

**Action framework:** If you believe the LNG re-rating thesis: long MU with Baker-style put hedge. If you don't: wait for the cycle trough (2027-2028) when P/E will appear absurdly high on collapsed earnings — that is historically the best entry. Baker's own position ($411M long + $200M puts) is the template for uncertainty.

### AVGO — The ASIC Beneficiary

**Why it matters for the long-term thesis:** Broadcom is the design partner for hyperscaler custom silicon. Every dollar that hyperscalers redirect from NVIDIA GPUs to custom ASICs flows through Broadcom's $53B ASIC backlog. Wang's thesis (NVDA margin compression from customer alternatives) is directly bullish AVGO.

**Structural advantage:** Hock Tan's supply discipline historically prevented the boom-bust cycle that hit peers. VMware's 93% gross margin software revenue (42% of total) provides ballast. Broadcom's blended 77.5% gross margin looks like NVIDIA's, but AVGO's is more defensible because the software mix doesn't face bilateral monopoly pressure.

**Risk:** AVGO is already priced as a secular compounder. Baker doesn't hold it — not enough cyclical mispricing for his framework. At current valuations, the upside is in the secular growth story (custom ASIC TAM 44% CAGR to 2030), which depends on assumptions about architectural stability that rapid model design shifts may undermine.

**Action framework:** Hold or gradually build. Not a "buy the cycle trough" play. Best entry is on any broad AI sentiment correction. AVGO earnings 2026-06-12.

## Tier 3: Monitor / Reduce Exposure

### NVDA — The Structural Short (Margin), Cyclical Hold (Revenue)

**The core problem:** NVIDIA faces three independent headwinds on a 2-3 year horizon:

1. **Margin compression (structural, ongoing):** Suppliers (TSMC, HBM vendors) reprice from below, customers build alternatives from above. GAAP gross margin already 75.0% → 71.1%. Street models 74% → 71% over 5 years, likely understating bilateral pressure. Each 100bps of margin compression on $200B+ revenue = $2B+ in operating income.

2. **Multiple compression (cyclical, underway):** Fwd P/E compressed from 60x+ to 21x. Baker's framework: the market refuses to capitalize shortage-level earnings at peak multiples. This is happening now, not later.

3. **Revenue deceleration risk (cyclical, 2027-2028):** Baker's "every shortage → glut" framework. NVDA's $95.2B supply commitments (doubled QoQ) = capacity locked even if demand slows. If the cycle turns, revenue decelerates while margins are already compressed.

**Counter-arguments (real ones):**
- CUDA moat remains strong for training. Software switching costs buy time.
- Revenue may grow from $215B to $300B+ even as margins compress. EPS can grow through top-line expansion.
- The CapEx prisoner's dilemma sustains pricing — no hyperscaler can unilaterally stop buying GPUs.
- Jensen's token economics: if every company becomes a token factory, demand may outgrow supply for longer than anyone expects.

**Baker vs. Leopold signal:** Baker is $1B+ long NVDA (highest conviction). Leopold exited entirely. This is the clearest thesis disagreement in the portfolio. Baker is explicitly betting the cycle extends longer; Leopold is betting downstream deployment captures more value. Wang's framework suggests Leopold may be right on the structural direction even if Baker is right on the near-term trade.

**Action framework:** NVDA is not a "short" — demand is too strong near-term. But it is the wrong name to build a long-term core position in. On a 2-3 year horizon, the risk/reward tilts negative:
- **Best case:** Revenue grows to $300B+, margins compress to low 60s, EPS grows modestly. Stock flat to up 20%.
- **Moderate case:** Revenue grows to $250B, margins compress to mid-60s, multiple stays at 20x. Stock flat.
- **Bear case:** Cyclical turn in 2027-2028 + margin compression. Revenue decelerates, margins at 60%, multiple compresses to 15x. Stock down 30-50%.

The asymmetry is unfavorable. Reduce position over the next 12 months. Rotate into Tier 1 names (TSM, ASML on drawdown) and Tier 2 (MU with hedge, AVGO).

### AMD — Second-Source Catalyst, Not a Core Position

AMD's MI355X is competitive on cost-per-token at high concurrency. Hyperscalers have structural motivation to buoy AMD as a second source to discipline NVIDIA pricing. But AMD faces the same supply chain (TSMC, HBM) and has lower margins (~44%). AMD benefits from the "NVIDIA margin compression" thesis as a BATNA provider, but captures less value than the infrastructure layers that serve everyone.

**Action framework:** Tactical, not core. AMD is a leverage play on NVDA margin compression, but TSM and ASML are better expressions of the same thesis with stronger structural positioning.

## The Rotation Sequence

Combining both frameworks into a time-phased positioning:

### Phase 1: Now through Q4 2026 (Peak Shortage)

The cycle is still running. Demand exceeds supply across memory, N3, and optics. Revenue is growing everywhere.

- **Hold:** Existing bottleneck positions (optics, memory, N3)
- **Build:** TSM core position (pricing power thesis, earnings 2026-04-17)
- **Begin reducing:** NVDA exposure (sell rallies, use to fund TSM/ASML positions)
- **Watch:** ASML bookings (2026-04-16 earnings) for early cycle-turn signals
- **Hedge:** Baker-style QQQ puts or NVDA put spreads as portfolio insurance

### Phase 2: H1 2027 (Cycle Turn Signals)

Baker's framework: stocks lead fundamentals by 6-12 months. If the cycle peaks in late 2026, stocks start falling H1 2027 while revenue is still growing.

- **Exit:** Remaining NVDA, tactical AMD
- **Reduce:** MU if LNG re-rating thesis hasn't materialized (unless hedged)
- **Hold:** TSM (structural, higher floor each cycle), AVGO (software ballast)
- **Prepare:** Cash / dry powder for Tier 1 buying opportunities in the downturn
- **Watch:** Equipment bookings (ASML, LRCX, AMAT), memory lead times, hyperscaler CapEx guidance

### Phase 3: 2027-2028 (Cyclical Downturn)

If Baker's framework plays out, this is the buying opportunity of the cycle.

- **Buy aggressively:** TSM on 25-40% drawdown (structural pricing power + higher AI revenue floor)
- **Buy aggressively:** ASML on 30-50% drawdown when bookings are falling (terminal monopoly at trough multiple)
- **Buy:** MU at high P/E on depressed earnings (Baker's counter-intuitive entry signal)
- **Hold:** AVGO through the drawdown (software revenue provides ballast)
- **Avoid:** NVDA unless margin stabilizes (wait for evidence that bilateral pressure has peaked)

## The Bottom Line

Right now, demand is sky high and everyone is printing. That is exactly when Baker says to worry about the cycle turn and Wang says to look at where the surplus is actually accruing.

The highest-conviction long-term call from combining both frameworks: **rotate out of the chip design layer (NVDA) and into the physical infrastructure layers (TSM, ASML) over the next 12 months.** TSMC and ASML benefit from every competitive outcome in the accelerator market, have under-exercised pricing power, and trade at structural discounts to their monopoly positions. NVIDIA faces margin compression that is structural (not cyclical) layered on top of the inevitable cyclical turn.

Memory (MU) is the swing factor. If long-term contracting genuinely breaks the DRAM cycle, it's the biggest re-rating in the stack. If it doesn't, it's the biggest drawdown. Baker's approach — long with put hedge — is the right way to express that uncertainty.

The meta-principle: **value accrues not to "who sells the most GPUs" but to "who captures the most surplus per dollar of AI infrastructure deployed."** On a 2-3 year horizon, that's TSMC (fabrication + packaging monopoly), the HBM oligopoly (if contracting holds), and ASML (terminal ceiling). Not NVIDIA.
