# Micron Q2 FY2026 Earnings

Micron's March 18, 2026 quarter is the clearest financial proof that [[concepts/memory-supercycle]] is not theoretical anymore. Revenue, margins, EPS, and free cash flow all set records, while management explicitly said DRAM and NAND demand remain constrained by supply and should stay tight beyond 2026.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Q2 FY2026 | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $23.86B | vs $13.64B in Q1 |
| GAAP gross margin | 74.4% | vs 56.0% in Q1 |
| Non-GAAP gross margin | 74.9% | vs 56.8% in Q1 |
| GAAP EPS | $12.07 | record |

| Capex | $5.0B | quarter |

| Q3 FY2026 revenue guide | $33.5B +/- $750M | record guide |
| Q3 FY2026 non-GAAP EPS guide | $19.15 +/- $0.40 | gross margin ~81% |

## Why It Matters

The trade-relevant points are straightforward:

- AI is pulling memory from a cyclical component into a strategic constraint.
- Micron says both AI and traditional server demand are constrained by inadequate DRAM and NAND supply.
- HBM4 shipments for NVIDIA Vera Rubin have already started, tying Micron directly into the 2026 GPU ramp.
- Management raised FY2026 capex above $25B because cleanroom and packaging capacity have become the bottleneck.

## Key Management Claims

- Data center DRAM and NAND bit TAM should exceed 50% of industry TAM for the first time in calendar 2026.
- DRAM and NAND supply-demand conditions should remain tight beyond calendar 2026.
- 1-gamma DRAM should become a majority of Micron's DRAM bit mix by mid-calendar 2026.
- Micron has begun HBM4 volume shipments for NVIDIA Vera Rubin.
- FY2026 capex is now expected to be above $25B.
