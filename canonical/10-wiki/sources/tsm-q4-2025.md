# TSMC Q4 2025 Earnings

TSMC's January 15, 2026 quarter is the base-layer proof for [[concepts/n3-wafer-crunch]]. Leading-edge demand remained strong, 3nm already accounted for 28% of wafer revenue, advanced nodes were 77% of wafer revenue, and management guided both another strong Q1 and a $52B-$56B 2026 capex budget.

## Why It Matters

TSMC sells the actual constrained substrate of the AI stack: advanced wafer starts and advanced packaging services. Every major AI accelerator roadmap ultimately terminates here.

The quarter confirms:

- leading-edge demand is still strong enough to push another up-quarter,
- advanced-node mix keeps rising,
- 2026 is still an expansion year rather than a digestion year,
- capex remains enormous because the supply response is expensive and slow.

## Key Management Claims

1. Q1 2026 revenue of $34.6B-$35.8B
2. Q1 2026 gross margin of 63%-65%
3. Q1 2026 operating margin of 54%-56%
4. 2026 capex of $52B-$56B
5. 2026 revenue should rise close to 30% in USD terms

## Fund Read-Through

Neither Leopold nor Baker holds TSM directly in the current 13F set, but both portfolios depend on TSM capacity:

- Baker through NVDA and other semiconductor supply-chain longs
- Leopold through INTC calls, downstream optics names, and GPU-cloud infrastructure that still needs advanced chips

The absence of direct TSM ownership is itself informative: both investors are trying to capture the bottleneck through more leveraged or more thesis-expressive downstream exposures.
