# Coherent Q2 FY2026 Earnings

Coherent printed another strong AI-optics quarter on February 4, 2026. Datacenter and communications remained the growth engine, now 72% of revenue, while management guided to continued strength in the second half of FY2026 and FY2027 as production capacity expands.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Q2 FY2026 | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $1.69B | +17% YoY reported, +22% pro forma |
| GAAP gross margin | 36.9% | +145bps YoY |
| Non-GAAP gross margin | 39.0% | +77bps YoY |
| GAAP EPS | $0.76 | +71% YoY |
| Non-GAAP EPS | $1.29 | +35% YoY |
| Datacenter & Communications | $1.208B | 72% of revenue |
| Industrial | $478M | 28% of revenue |
| Q3 FY2026 revenue guide | $1.70B-$1.84B | Includes $5M from sold Munich tools business |
| Q3 FY2026 non-GAAP EPS guide | $1.28-$1.48 | Margin guide 38.5%-40.5% |

## Why It Matters

Coherent is one of the cleanest current expressions of [[concepts/pluggable-optics]] and one of the better-positioned names for [[concepts/co-packaged-optics]]. The quarter matters because:

- Datacenter and communications is now the overwhelming majority of the business.
- Management is explicitly tying the outlook to continued capacity expansion.
- March 2026 follow-on artifacts show Coherent is not only selling current optical components, but also moving deeper into AI-specific CPO and silicon photonics stacks.

## AI Optics Follow-Through After Earnings

- NVIDIA and Coherent announced a multiyear strategic agreement on March 18, 2026, including an NVIDIA multibillion-dollar purchase commitment, future capacity rights, and a $2B NVIDIA investment to expand Coherent's U.S. manufacturing and R&D.
- At OFC 2026, Coherent highlighted 6.4T socketed CPO, silicon photonics, VCSEL-based CPO, 400G-per-lane InP-on-silicon modulation, and 1.6T/XPO-related optical building blocks.

This matters because it reduces the risk that Coherent is just a generic optics beta. The evidence points to a broader position across pluggables, lasers, photonic engines, and future package-level optics.
