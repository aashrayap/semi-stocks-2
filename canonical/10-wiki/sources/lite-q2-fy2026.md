# Lumentum Q2 FY2026 Earnings

Lumentum's February 3, 2026 quarter is one of the strongest current proofs that AI optics revenue is real, not just an architecture story. Revenue grew 65.5% YoY, management guided to another step up in Q3, and the company explicitly said OCS backlog is already above $400M while CPO orders are now multi-hundred-million-dollar sized.

## Why It Matters

Lumentum now matters across both:

- [[concepts/pluggable-optics]]: current revenue, component shipments, and optical circuit switching
- [[concepts/co-packaged-optics]]: explicit CPO orders, NVIDIA partnership, and future package-level integration

This quarter reduces the "future optionality only" objection. Revenue and margin are already moving.

## AI Optics Follow-Through

- Management said OCS backlog is well beyond $400M and customer demand remains extraordinary.
- Management disclosed an incremental multi-hundred-million-dollar CPO order for delivery in 1H CY2027.
- NVIDIA announced a multiyear agreement with Lumentum plus a $2B investment, future capacity rights, and a multibillion purchase commitment for advanced laser components.
- The R64 OCS platform extends the OCS line into lower-port-count AI data-center deployments, scales to 72x72 ports, cuts power materially versus packet switches, and is expected to be generally available in 2H 2026.
