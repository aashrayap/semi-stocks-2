Where we are: The semiconductor supply chain has a sequence of physical constraints that limit how fast AI can scale. Each one gets solved eventually, but the next one becomes the new ceiling. Right now, Memory supercycle and N3 logic wafers and Pluggable optics (scale-out) are the active constraints — they're happening simultaneously, not sequentially as originally expected.

What the money says: Leopold Aschenbrenner's fund ($5.5B) and Gavin Baker's fund ($8.2B) are both betting heavily on AI infrastructure but disagree on where the constraint is tightest. Leopold has exited chip companies entirely and loaded up on power infrastructure and GPU cloud operators. Baker has done the opposite — $1B+ in Nvidia alone — betting that chips themselves remain scarce. The cleanest three-source overlap is optical interconnect (LITE, COHR), which makes it the highest-conviction, lowest-divergence-risk zone in the entire thesis.

Cycle risk: Memory supercycle is at peak shortage — Baker's framework says hedge, don't exit. His QQQ put hedge ratio: 70% (trend: increasing).
