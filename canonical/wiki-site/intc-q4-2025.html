Intel’s January 22, 2026 quarter is not a clean bull case, but it is an important bottleneck datapoint. The numbers show AI-related server demand is strong enough that Intel still cannot fully meet it, while 18A and foundry ramp represent one of the only plausible future supply responses to the current leading-edge crunch.

Why It Matters

Intel matters to concepts/n3-wafer-crunch in two opposing ways:

  • It confirms the current shortage because Intel said revenue would have been meaningfully higher with more supply and that AI infrastructure demand is pressuring wafers and components.
  • It partially contradicts the shortage over time because Intel is ramping 18A, pushing EUV mix higher, and trying to become a credible additional source of advanced logic and packaging capacity.

That makes INTC more thesis-divergent than COHR or MU. It is both evidence of present scarcity and a possible future release valve.

Key Management Claims

  1. Q1 2026 revenue of $11.7B-$12.7B with the sharpest supply constraints in Q1
  2. Intel Foundry revenue should rise double digits QoQ in Q1 on EUV and 18A mix
  3. Available supply should improve beginning in Q2 and through the rest of 2026
  4. 2026 opex target is $16B
  5. 2026 capex should be flat to down slightly, weighted to the first half
  6. 14A supplier decisions should begin in H2 2026 and continue into H1 2027
